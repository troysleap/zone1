🏛️ Our Area

Zone 1 covers the back stage area and the east hallway — and honestly, out of all the assignments, we got a great one! 🎉 The Grantville Assembly Hall was just beautifully remodeled, and our congregation has the privilege of caring for one of its nicest areas. Fresh paint, new finishes — let's keep it shining!

That's us in orange — back stage at the top, and the east hallway running down the right side. One heads-up: the video operations room is not ours to clean — please leave it be.

🧽 What We Do

✅ Our Zone 1 checklist

  • Touch surfaces — door handles, knobs, light switches, etc.
  • Mirrors and glass.
  • Remove trash, clean receptacles, and replace all liners.
  • Furniture.
  • Vacuum carpet — the Tornado wide-area vacuum is used for the hallway. It's a two-person job (one drives, one manages the cord) and it goes in one direction only, like mowing a lawn. 🚜

🔵 🟡 🔴 Know your rag colors!

Rags are color-coded so the right rag always goes with the right job:

  • Blue rags — glass only.
  • Yellow rags — general use: doors, handles, dispensers, sinks.
  • Red rags — high-risk: toilets, urinals, trash receptacles, changing tables. Red never touches anything else.

When you're done, bring soiled towels to the Cleaning Desk sorted by color — and please don't wash any towels or mop heads yourself; the Assembly Hall handles all laundry. Not sure which rag? Just ask!

🧴 Only two products

  • 3M 40L germicide — every surface except glass, mirrors, and floors.
  • Peroxy cleaner — glass, mirrors, and mopping floors (dispensers are in the bathroom cleaning closets).

🗑️ Trash runs

  • Dumpsters are at the rear of the building by the loading dock — use the South exit through the Multipurpose Room, and wear a Hi-Vis safety vest (they're in the Coat Room; please return it at day's end).
  • Never carry trash bags across carpet — use the black trash gondola through the loading dock.
  • Recycling dumpster gets contents only: empty the bag in, then the plastic bag/liner itself goes in the trash dumpster.

🦺 Staying Safe — Safety Is Spirituality

Caring for our safety and our brothers' and sisters' safety is part of our worship — so these matter just as much as a shiny floor:

  • Trash bags — please never press or smash trash down to make room or to empty a can. You never know what's inside — it could be something sharp. Lift bags gently by the top, away from your body, and if a bag is too full or too heavy, grab a second set of hands.
  • Wet floors: always put out a wet-floor sign before and after mopping.
  • Lifting: lift with your legs, and grab a friend for anything heavy — team lifts only.
  • Gloves: wear gloves when handling trash bags.
  • Reach, don't climb: only clean what you can reach — no ladders or stepstools are available for use, and that's on purpose.
  • Cords: don't run over equipment cords with the vacuum, and keep extension cords out of walkways.
  • Anything unusual: leaks, spills, broken glass, or anything that doesn't look right — just find Troy or the Assembly Overseer. You never need to handle it alone.
